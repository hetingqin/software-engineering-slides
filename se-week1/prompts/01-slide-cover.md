## STYLE_INSTRUCTIONS

Design Aesthetic: Hand-drawn sketch-note feel with soft organic textures, warm earth-tone colors, and handwritten-style typography. Creates an approachable, educational atmosphere like a professor's notebook or a beautifully illustrated lecture handout. Balanced density with 2-3 key points per slide.

Background:
  Texture: Soft off-white paper with subtle grain, reminiscent of a premium notebook or sketchpad
  Base Color: Warm off-white (#F5F0E8)

Typography:
  Headlines: Bold, expressive handwritten marker style with slight irregularity, thick strokes, as if drawn with a chisel-tip marker
  Body: Clean rounded handwritten style, lighter weight, highly legible, as if written with a fine-tip pen

Color Palette:
  Primary Text: Charcoal (#2D3436) - headlines and main text
  Background: Warm Off-White (#F5F0E8) - slide background
  Accent 1: Terracotta (#E17055) - key concepts, highlights, important markers
  Accent 2: Teal (#00B894) - secondary highlights, icons, diagrams
  Accent 3: Warm Gold (#FDCB6E) - background fills, callout boxes
  Accent 4: Soft Blue (#74B9FF) - supporting elements, arrows, connectors

Visual Elements:
  - Hand-drawn icons and doodles with organic, slightly imperfect lines
  - Sketch-style diagrams with arrows, boxes, and flow lines
  - Marker-style underlines and highlights
  - Banner-style headings with hand-drawn ribbons
  - Dashed connection lines and hand-drawn arrows

Density Guidelines:
  - Content per slide: 2-3 key points maximum
  - Whitespace: 30-40% of slide area
  - Elements: 3-5 visual elements per slide

Style Rules:
  Do: Allow slight imperfections in lines and shapes; use organic curves; layer elements with subtle overlaps; use warm tones throughout; make diagrams look hand-sketched; use marker-style emphasis
  Don't: Use perfect geometric shapes; use photorealistic elements; use cold/sterile colors; overcrowd slides; use computer-generated clean fonts
